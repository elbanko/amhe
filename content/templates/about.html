About Joe Ankus



Joe is a Florida attorney whose MHFA mission is both personal and professional- since 1991, he has been a legal recruiter for many of South Florida's prominent law firms after having worked with two AmLaw 100 firms- Dechert and Holland & Knight. He knows, firsthand, the pressures and stresses attorneys face on a daily basis. He has lived the “billable grind” and understands the lifestyles that attorneys often lead. He has credibility to teach attorneys MHFA because he is one himself.

Joe has successfully placed over 500 attorneys in his career and he believes that MHFA education correlates directly to his recruiting practice and as a lawyer.

Most recently, he was a member of the Florida Bar's Special Committee on Mental Health & Wellness whose mandate- in part- was to offer solutions to educate Florida attorneys on mental health education. On the Committee, Joe worked closely with members of the Judiciary, Bar staff, a psychiatrist, and other passionate attorneys in "moving the needle" to address the growing need for attorney mental health related education. After some research, Joe realized MHFA was a logical choice.

In his experience as both an attorney and as a recruiter, he has seen first-hand the urgent need for an evidence based, concise and user-friendly format for basic mental health education. Attorneys, as a profession, are under significant stress, have competing demands for their time and often untrained in basic mental health education. This gap needs to be addressed and MHFA is one part of a multi-faceted solution. Joe believes that the earlier MHFA is taught, the better off the overall legal community will be.
